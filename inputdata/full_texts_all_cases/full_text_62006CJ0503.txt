
Judgment of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of May – Commission v Italy
(Case C‑503/06)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 79/409/EEC – Conservation of wild birds – Derogations from the system of protection of wild birds – Region of Liguria
1.                     Member States – Obligations – Implementation of directives – Failure to fulfil obligations not contested (Art. EC) (see
            paras 22-23)
2.                     Environment – Conservation of wild birds – Directive 79/– Implementation by Member States (Art. EC; Council Directive
            79/409, Art. 9) (see para. 24)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Adoption and application by the Region of Liguria of legislation authorising
                  derogations from the system of protection for wild birds which fails to satisfy the conditions laid down in Article of Council
                  Directive 79/409/EEC of April concerning the conservation of wild birds (OJ L 103, p. 1).
               
Operative part 
The Court:
1.
	Declares that, by adopting and applying, for the region of Liguria, legislation authorising derogations from the system of
                     protection for wild birds which fails to satisfy the conditions laid down in Article of Council Directive 79/409/EEC of
                     April concerning the conservation of wild birds, the Italian Republic has failed to fulfil its obligations under that
                     directive;
                  
2.
	Orders the Italian Republic to bear the costs, including those linked to the interlocutory proceedings.
