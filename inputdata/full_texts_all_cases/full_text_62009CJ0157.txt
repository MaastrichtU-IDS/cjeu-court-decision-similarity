
Judgment of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of December – Commission v Netherlands
(Case C-157/09)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Article EC – Freedom of establishment – Notaries – Condition of nationality – Article EC – Connection with the exercise of official authority
Freedom of movement for persons – Freedom of establishment – Freedom to provide services – Derogations – Activities connected with the exercise of official authority – Notarial activities – Excluded – Condition of nationality for access to the profession of notary – Unlawful (Arts EC and 45, first para., EC) (see paras 55, 57-58, 60-65, 67-69, 73-74, 76-78, 80-86, 90)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Infringement of Articles EC and EC – Access to and practice of the
                  profession of notary – Condition of nationality – Connection with the exercise of official authority.
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by imposing a condition of nationality for access to the profession of notary, the Kingdom of the Netherlands
                     has failed to fulfil its obligations under Article EC; 
                  
2.
	Orders the Kingdom of the Netherlands to pay the costs;
3.
	Orders the Republic of Slovenia to bear its own costs.
