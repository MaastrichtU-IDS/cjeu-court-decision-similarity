
Order of the Court (Fifth Chamber) of May – X Technology Swiss v OHIM
(Case C-429/P)
Appeal – Community trade mark – Sign consisting of the partial colouring of a product – Orange colouring of the toe of a sock – Absolute ground for refusal – Absence of distinctive character – Regulation (EC) No 40/– Article 7(1)(b)
Appeals – Grounds – Incorrect assessment of the facts and evidence – Inadmissibility – Review by the Court of the assessment of the facts and evidence – Possible only where the clear sense of the evidence has been distorted (Art. 256(1) TFEU; Statute of the Court of Justice,
         Art. 58(1)) (see para 41)
      
Re 
Appeal against the judgment of the General Court (Second Chamber) of June in Case T-547/X Technology Swiss
 v 
OHIM
, by which that court dismissed the action brought against the decision of the Fourth Board of Appeal of OHIM of October
                  dismissing the appeal against the examiner’s decision, which refused registration of the sign consisting of the orange
                  colouring of the toe of a sock as a Community trade mark for goods in Class – Distinctive character of a sign consisting
                  of the partial colouring of a product.
               
Operative part
1.
	The appeal is dismissed.
2.
	X Technology Swiss GmbH is ordered to pay the costs.
