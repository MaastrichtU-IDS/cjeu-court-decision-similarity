
Judgment of the Court (First Chamber) of June – Commission v Austria
(Case C‑356/08)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Freedom to provide services – Freedom of establishment – Free movement of capital – National legislation imposing an obligation on medical doctors established in the territory of the Land of Upper Austria
         to open a bank account with a particular bank
      
1.                     Freedom of movement for persons – Freedom of establishment – Freedom to provide services – Provisions of the Treaty – Scope
            (Arts EC and EC) (see para. 37)
2.                     Freedom to provide services – Restrictions – Rules of the national medical association imposing on its members established
            in the national territory the obligation to open a bank account with a particular bank  (Art. EC) (see paras 40-49, 51,
            operative part 1)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Breach of Articles EC, EC and EC – National legislation imposing
                  an obligation on medical doctors established in the territory of the 
               
Land
 of Upper Austria to open a bank account with the Oberösterreichische Landesbank.
Operative part
The Court:
1.
	Declares that, by imposing an obligation on every medical doctor becoming established in Oberösterreich (Land of Upper Austria)
                     to open a bank account with the Oberösterreichische Landesbank in Linz to which fees for benefits in kind in the context of
                     the exercise of his professional activity are to be transferred by the health insurance funds, the Republic of Austria has
                     failed to comply with its obligations under Article EC;
                  
2.
	Orders the Republic of Austria to pay the costs.
