
Judgment of the Court (Seventh Chamber) of December – Commission v Luxembourg
(Case C‑223/06)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 2003/51/EC – Company law – Annual accounts of certain types of companies – Failure to implement within the prescribed period
Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see para. 8)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Failure to have adopted, within the prescribed period, all the provisions
                  necessary to comply with Directive 2003/51/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of June amending Council
                  Directives 78/660/EEC, 83/349/EEC, 86/635/EEC and 91/674/EEC on the annual and consolidated accounts of certain types of companies,
                  banks and other financial institutions and insurance undertakings (OJ L 178, p. 16).
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
Declares that, by failing to adopt, within the prescribed period, the laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary
                     to comply with Directive 2003/51/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of June amending Council Directives
                     78/660/EEC, 83/349/EEC, 86/635/EEC and 91/674/EEC on the annual and consolidated accounts of certain types of companies, banks
                     and other financial institutions and insurance undertakings, the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg has failed to fulfil its obligations
                     under that directive;
                  
2.
Orders the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg to pay the costs.
