
Judgment of the Court (Fourth Chamber) of June – Commission v Ireland
(Case C‑148/05)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil its obligations – Directive 79/923/EEC – Quality of shellfish waters – Designation of shellfish waters – Pollution reduction programmes – Setting control parameters
1.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Pre-litigation procedure – Formal notice (Art. EC) (see para. 32)
2.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Subject-matter of the dispute – Determination during the procedure prior to the
            action (Art. EC) (see paras 34-35)
3.                     Approximation of laws – Quality required of shellfish waters – Directive 79/(Council Directive 79/923, Arts and 4) (see
            paras 46-48, 61-66)
Re:
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Incomplete transposition of Articles 3, and of Council Directive 79/923/EEC
                  of October on the quality required of shellfish waters (OJ L 281, p. 47) – Failure to designate certain shellfish
                  waters, to establish pollution-reduction programmes and to set control parameters.
               
Operative part
The Court:
 
Declares that, by failing:
–       in accordance with Article of Council Directive 79/923/EEC of October on the quality required of shellfish waters,
         to designate all shellfish waters requiring designation;
      
–       in accordance with Article of Directive 79/923, to set all the required values in respect of shellfish waters designated
         or requiring designation pursuant to Article 4; and
      
–       in accordance with Article of Directive 79/923, to take all necessary measures to establish pollution reduction programmes
         for waters requiring designation pursuant to Article 4, 
      
Ireland has failed to fulfil its obligations under that directive;
 
Orders Ireland to pay the costs.
