
Judgment of the Court (Seventh Chamber) of March – Commission v France
(Case C‑423/05)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Waste management – Directives 75/442/EEC and 1999/31/EC – Unlawful or unsupervised landfill sites
Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see point 11)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Infringement of Articles 4, and of Council Directive 75/442/EEC of July (OJ L 194, p. 39) on waste, as amended by Council Directive 91/156/EEC of March amending Directive
                  75/442/EEC on waste (OJ L 78, p. 32) and Article 14(a), (b) and (c) of Council Directive 1999/31/EC of April on the landfill of waste (OJ L 182, p. 1) – Failure to take necessary measures to close down or upgrade unlawful or unsupervised
                  landfill sites.
               
Operative part
 
By failing to adopt all the laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary to comply with Articles 4, and of
                     Council Directive 75/442/EEC of July on waste, as amended by Council Directive 91/156/EEC of March 1991, and Article
                     14(a), (b) and (c) of Council Directive 1999/31/EC of April on the landfill of waste, the French Republic has failed
                     to fulfil its obligations under those provisions.
                  
 
The French Republic is ordered to pay the costs.
