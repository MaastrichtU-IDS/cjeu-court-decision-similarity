
Order of the Court (Fifth Chamber) of February – Lebedef v Commission
(Case C 268/P)
Appeal – Officials – Rules concerning the levels, process and procedure of consultation agreed by the majority of trade union and professional
         organisations and the Commission – Exclusion of the ‘Action & Défense’ union – Manifestly inadmissible
      
Appeals – Grounds of appeal – Mere repetition of the pleas and arguments put forward before the Court of First Instance – Inadmissible (Art. EC; Statute of the Court of Justice, Art. 58, first para.; Rules of Procedure of the Court, Art. 112(1)(c))
         (see paras 13-15)
      
Re:
Appeal against the judgment delivered on April by the Court of First Instance (First Chamber) in Case T-191/Lebedef
 v 
Commission
, by which it dismissed an application for annulment of the Commission’s decision by which it repudiated the Framework Agreement
                  of September on the relations between the Commission and the trade union and professional organisations, adopted again
                  the operational rules concerning the levels, process and procedure of consultation agreed by the Commission and the majority
                  of trade union and professional organisations on January 2000, which had been annulled by the Court of First Instance in
                  its judgment of November 2001, in so far as they excluded from the process of consultation the union ‘Action et Défense’
               
Operative part:
The Court: 
1.
Dismisses the appeal;
2.
Orders Mr Lebedef to pay the costs.
