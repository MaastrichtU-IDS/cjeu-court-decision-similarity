
Judgment of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of June – Commission v Sweden
(Case C‑333/06)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Regulation (EC) No 261/– Air transport – Denied boarding and cancellation or long delays of flights – Compensation and assistance to passengers – Adoption of sanctions
Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see para. 10)
Re:
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Failure to take the measures necessary to comply with Article of Regulation
                  (EC) No 261/of the European Parliament and of the Council of February establishing common rules on compensation
                  and assistance to passengers in the event of denied boarding and of cancellation or long delays of flights, and repealing
                  Regulation (EEC) No 295/(OJ L 46, p. 1) – Laying down of effective, proportionate and dissuasive sanctions.
               
Operative part
The Court:
 
Declares that, by failing to lay down the sanctions for infringements of the provisions of Regulation (EC) No 261/of
                     the European Parliament and of the Council of February establishing common rules on compensation and assistance to
                     passengers in the event of denied boarding and of cancellation or long delays of flights, and repealing Regulation (EEC) No
                     295/91, the Kingdom of Sweden has failed to fulfil its obligations under Article of that directive;
                  
 
Dismisses the remainder of the action;
 
Orders the Kingdom of Sweden to pay the costs.
