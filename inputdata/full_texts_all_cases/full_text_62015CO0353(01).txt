Order of the Court (Seventh Chamber) of 24 May 2016 —

Leonmobili and Leone

(Case C‑353/15) ( 1 )

‛Reference for a preliminary ruling — Regulation (EC) No 1346/2000 — Article 3(1) and (2) — Insolvency proceedings — International jurisdiction — Centre of a debtor’s main interests — Transfer of a company’s registered office to another Member State — No establishment in the Member State of origin — Presumption that the centre of main interests is the place of the new registered office — Proof to the contrary’

3. 

Judicial cooperation in civil matters — Insolvency proceedings — Regulation No 1346/2000 — International jurisdiction to open insolvency proceedings — Courts of the Member State in which the centre of the debtor’s main interests is situated — Centre of a debtor’s main interests — Independent concept of EU law (Council Regulation No 1346/2000, Art. 3(1)) (see para. 32)

4. 

Judicial cooperation in civil matters — Insolvency proceedings —Regulation No 1346/2000 — International jurisdiction to open insolvency proceedings — Courts of the Member State in which the centre of the debtor’s main interests is situated — Criteria for determining that State — Presumption in favour of the place where companies have their registered office — Rebuttable presumption — Need for objective information verifiable by third parties — Transfer of the registered office of the debtor company before a request to open insolvency proceedings is lodged — Presumption in favour of the new registered office (Council Regulation No 1346/2000, Art. 3(1)) (see paras 33-41, operative part)

Operative part

Article 3(1) of Council regulation (EC) No 1346/2000 of 29 May 2000 on insolvency proceedings must be interpreted as meaning that, where a company’s registered office has been transferred from one Member State to another Member State, the court seised, subsequent to that transfer, of an application to open insolvency proceedings in the Member State of origin may disregard the presumption that that company’s centre of main interests is situated at the place of the new registered office and find that the centre of those interests remained, as at the date on which that application was brought before it, in that Member State of origin, although the company no longer had an establishment there, only if it is apparent from other objective evidence, ascertainable by third parties, that, nonetheless, the company’s actual centre of management and supervision and of the management of its interests was still located there at that date.
