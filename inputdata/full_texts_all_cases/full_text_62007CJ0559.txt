
Judgment of the Court (Third Chamber) of March – Commission v Greece
(Case C‑559/07)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Social policy – Article EC – Equal pay for male and female workers – National civil and military pension regime – Different treatment with regard to retirement age and minimum required service – Justification – None
1.                     Social policy – Male and female workers – Equal pay – Article EC – Effects of a judgment of the Court finding there to
            be discrimination (Art. EC) (see para. 26)
2.                     Social policy – Male and female workers – Equal pay – Remuneration –Meaning – Pensions regime for officials and civil servants
            (Art. EC) (see paras 42, 44, 46-47, 49-50, 55, 60)
3.                     Social policy – Male and female workers – Equal pay – Pensions regime for officials and civil servants (Art. EC) (see
            paras 62, 66-68)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Infringement of Article EC – Infringement of the principle of equal
                  pay for male and female workers – National civil and military retirement pensions regime prescribing a retirement age that
                  can vary according to sex.
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by maintaining in force provisions which provide for differences between male and female workers with regard
                     to retirement age and minimum required service under the Greek Civil and Military Pensions Code instituted by Presidential
                     Decree No 166/of July 2000, in the version applicable to the present case, the Hellenic Republic has failed to fulfil
                     its obligations under Article EC;
                  
2.
	Orders the Hellenic Republic to pay the costs.
