
Judgment of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of March – Commission v Belgium
(Case C‑342/08)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 96/82/EC – Article 11(1)(c) – Failure to draw up external emergency plans – Incomplete transposition
1.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of the merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration
            – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see para. 12)
2.                     Member States – Obligations – Implementation of directives – Failure to fulfil obligations – National system pleaded as justification
            – Not permissible ( Art. EC) (see para. 13)
3.                     Acts of the institutions – Directives – Implementation by Member States – Need to ensure that directives are effective (Art.
            EC) (see para. 16)
Re:
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Failure to draw up external emergency plans for the measures to be taken
                  outside establishments falling within Article of Council Directive 96/82/EC of December on the control of major-accident
                  hazards involving dangerous substances (OJ L 10, p. 13).
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by failing to ensure that an external emergency plan is drawn up for all the establishments covered by Article
                     of Council Directive 96/82/EC of December on the control of major-accident hazards involving dangerous substances,
                     as amended by Directive 2003/105/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of December 2003, the Kingdom of Belgium
                     has failed to fulfil its obligations under that directive. 
                  
2.
	Orders the Kingdom of Belgium to pay the costs.
