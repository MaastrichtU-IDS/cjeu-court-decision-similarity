
Judgment of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of October – Commission v Greece
(Case C‑36/08)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 93/16/EEC – Specific training required to practise as a general practitioner – Incorrect transposition
1.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Subject-matter of the dispute – Request seeking to have a Member State ordered
            to take specific measures (Art. EC) (see paras 8-10)
2.                     Freedom of movement for persons – Freedom of establishment – Freedom to provide services – Doctors – Directive 93/(Council
            Directive 93/16, Arts 30, and 36) (see operative part)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Infringement of Articles 30, and of Council Directive 93/16/EEC of
                  April to facilitate the free movement of doctors and the mutual recognition of their diplomas, certificates and other
                  evidence of formal qualifications (OJ L 165, p. 1) – Specific training required to practise as a general practitioner.
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by adopting and maintaining in force rules such as Article 29(d.1) and (d.2) of Law 3209/which are not
                     in compliance with Articles 30, and of Council Directive 93/16/EEC of April to facilitate the free movement of
                     doctors and the mutual recognition of their diplomas, certificates and other evidence of formal qualifications, as amended
                     by Directive 2001/19/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of May 2001, the Hellenic Republic has failed to
                     fulfil its obligations under Articles 30, and of that directive.
                  
2.
	Dismisses the action as to the remainder.
3.
	Orders the Hellenic Republic to pay the costs.
