
Judgment of the Court (Seventh Chamber) of September – Commission v Belgium
(Case C‑36/10)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil its obligations – Directives 96/82/EC and 2003/105/EC – Control of major-accident hazards involving dangerous substances – Second subparagraph of Article 12(1) – Incorrect transposition
Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of the merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. TFEU; Council Directive 96/82, Art. 12(1),
         para. 2) (see paras 10-12)
      
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil its obligations – Failure to adopt all the measures necessary in order to comply with
                  Council Directive 96/82/EC of December on the control of major-accident hazards involving dangerous substances (OJ
                  L 10, p. 13), as amended by Directive 2003/105/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of December (OJ
                  L 345, p. 97).
               
Operative part
The Court:
1.
	Declares that, by failing to adopt within the prescribed period all the measures to transpose correctly the second subparagraph
                     of Article 12(1) of Council Directive 96/82/EC of December on the control of major-accident hazards involving dangerous
                     substances, as amended by Directive 2003/105/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of December 2003, the Kingdom
                     of Belgium has failed to fulfil its obligations under that directive; 
                  
2.
	Orders the Kingdom of Belgium to pay the costs.
