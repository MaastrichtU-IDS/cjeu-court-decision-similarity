
Order of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of September —
Romonta v Commission
(Case C‑565/P) (
*)
‛Appeal — Environment — Directive 2003/87/EC — Article 10a — Scheme for greenhouse gas emission allowance trading — Transitional rules for harmonised free allocation of emission allowances from — Decision 2011/278/EU — National implementation measures submitted by the Federal Republic of Germany — Rejection of the registration of certain installations on the lists of installations receiving free allocations of emission allowances — Provision relating to cases of ‘undue hardship’ — Implementing powers of the Commission’
1. 
Appeal — Interest in bringing proceedings — Condition — Appeal capable of procuring an advantage for the party bringing it — Admissibility of a claim seeking substitution of grounds which amounts to a defence to one of the opposing party’s pleas (Art. TFEU; Statute of the Court of Justice, Art. 56, second para.) (see paras 34-36)
2. 
Acts of the institutions — Basic legislation and implementing legislation — Implementing legislation not capable of amending or supplementing the essential elements of the basic legislation — Classification of the essential elements — Taking account of the characteristics and particular features of the field concerned (Art. TFEU)(see paras 39, 40)
3. 
Environment — Atmospheric pollution — Directive 2003/— Aim — Reduction of greenhouse gas emissions — Compliance with the sub‑objectives defined by the directive (European Parliament and Council Directive 2003/87, as amended by Directive 2009/29, Recitals and and Art. 1) (see para. 41)
4. 
Environment — Atmospheric pollution — Directive 2003/— Scheme for greenhouse gas emission allowance trading — Transitional regime for free allocation of quotas — Fully harmonised allocation method on a sector specific basis — Implementing rules which may not go against that allocation method (European Parliament and Council Directive 2003/87, as amended by Directive 2009/29, Art. 10a(1)) (see paras 42, 44-47)
Operative part
1. 
The appeal is dismissed.
2. 
Romonta GmbH is ordered to bear its own costs and to pay those incurred by the European Commission.
(
*)	OJ C 46, 9.2.2015.
