
Order of the Court (Fifth Chamber) of April – Focus Magazin Verlag v OHIM and Merant 
(Case C‑344/P)
Appeal – Community trade mark – Regulation (EC) No 40/– Article 8(1)(b) – Likelihood of confusion – Word sign ‘FOCUS’
Appeals – Pleas in law – Incorrect assessment of the evidence – Inadmissibility – Review by the Court of Justice of the assessment of the evidence – Possible only where the clear sense of the evidence has been distorted (Art. 225(1) EC; Statute of the Court of Justice,
         Art. 58, first para.) (see para. 49)
      
Re: 
Appeal against the judgment of the Court of First Instance (Third Chamber) of May in Case T-491/Merant
 v 
OHIM
, by which that Court annulled decision R 542/2002-of the Second Board of Appeal of the Office for Harmonisation in the
                  internal Market (Trade Marks and Designs) (OHIM) of October upholding an action against the opposition decision which
                  partially rejected the application for registration of Community word mark ‘FOCUS’ for goods and services in classes 3, 6,
                  7, 8, 9, 14, 15, 16, 21, 24, 25, 26, 28, 29, 32, 33, 34, 35, 36, 38, 39, and in opposition proceedings brought by the
                  proprietor of the national figurative mark ‘MICRO FOCUS’ for goods and services in classes 9, 16, and – Likelihood of
                  confusion between two marks.
               
Operative part
The Court:
1.
	Dismisses the appeal;
2.
	Orders Focus Magazin Verlag GmbH to pay the costs.
