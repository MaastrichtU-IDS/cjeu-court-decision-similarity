
Judgment of the Court (Third Chamber) of July – Commission v Spain
(Case C‑207/07)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Articles EC and EC – National law making the acquisition of shareholdings in undertakings which carry on regulated activities in the energy sector
         and of the assets necessary to carry on those activities subject to prior approval
      
Freedom of movement for persons – Freedom of establishment – Free movement of capital – Energy law (Arts EC and EC) (see paras 39, 46, 54-55, 58, 62‑63, operative part)
Re:
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Breach of Articles EC and EC – National law making the acquisition
                  of certain shareholdings in undertakings which carry on regulated activities in the energy sector subject to prior approval
                  of a special commission.
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by adopting the first indent of the second paragraph of the single article of the fourteenth function of the
                     National Energy Commission provided for in Supplementary Provision No 11, part 3, point 1, of Law 34/of October on the hydrocarbon sector (Ley 34/1998, del sector de hidrocarburos), as amended by Royal Decree-Law 4/of February
                     (Real Decreto-Ley 4/2006), in order to make the acquisition of certain shareholdings in undertakings which carry on certain
                     regulated activities in the energy sector and the acquisition of the assets necessary to carry on such activities subject
                     to the prior approval of the National Energy Commission, the Kingdom of Spain has failed to fulfil its obligations under Articles
                     EC and EC;
                  
2.
	Orders the Kingdom of Spain to pay the costs.
