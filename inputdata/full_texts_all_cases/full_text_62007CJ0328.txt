
Judgment of the Court (Fifth Chamber) of February – Commission v Luxembourg
(Case C‑328/07)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 2004/48/EC – Enforcement of intellectual property rights – Failure to transpose within the prescribed period
1.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration
            – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see para. 9)
2.                     Member States – Obligations – Implementation of directives – Failure to fulfil obligations – National system pleaded as justification
            – Not permissible (Art. EC) (see para. 11)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Failure to adopt the provisions necessary to comply with Directive 2004/48/EC
                  of the European Parliament and of the Council of April on the enforcement of intellectual property rights (OJ L 157, p. 45, and corrigendum OJ L 195, p. 16).
               
Operative part
The Court:
1.
	Declares that, by failing to adopt, within the prescribed period, the laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary
                     to comply with Directive 2004/48/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of April on the enforcement of intellectual
                     property rights, the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg has failed to fulfil its obligations under that directive;
                  
2.
	Orders the Grand Duchy of Luxembourg to pay the costs.
