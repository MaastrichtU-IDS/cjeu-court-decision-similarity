
Judgment of the Court (Third Chamber) of March – 
Commission v Spain
(Case C‑248/06)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Freedom of establishment – Freedom to provide services – Restrictions – Research and development – Rules for the deduction of costs incurred abroad
Freedom of movement for persons – Freedom of establishment – Freedom to provide services – Restrictions – Tax legislation – Corporation tax (Arts EC and EC; EEA Agreement, Arts and 36) (see paras 25, 42-43, operative part)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Infringement of Articles EC, EC and EC and Articles EEA and
                  EEA – Rules for deducting costs for research and development and technological innovation incurred abroad more rigorous
                  than those applicable to costs incurred in Spain.
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by maintaining in force rules for the deduction of costs relating to research and development and technological
                     innovation which are less favourable in respect of costs incurred abroad than costs incurred in Spain – such rules deriving
                     from Article of the Law on corporation tax, as amended by Royal Legislative Decree 4/of March – the Kingdom
                     of Spain has failed to fulfil its obligations under Articles EC and EC on freedom of establishment and the freedom to
                     provide services, and the corresponding articles in the Agreement on the European Economic Area of May 1992, namely Articles
                     and of that Agreement;
                  
2.
	Orders the Kingdom of Spain to pay the costs.
