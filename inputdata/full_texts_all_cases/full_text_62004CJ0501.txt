
Judgment of the Court (Third Chamber) of July – Commission v Spain
(Case C‑501/04)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Direct insurance other than life assurance and direct life assurance – Directives 92/49/EEC and 2002/83/EC – Portfolio transfer system – Right to cancel – Admissibility
1.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Application initiating proceedings – Statement of subject-matter and pleas in
            law – Formal requirements (Art. EC; Statute of the Court of Justice, Art. 21; Rules of Procedure of the Court of Justice,
            Art. 38(1)(c)) (see para. 23)
2.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Subject-matter of the dispute – Determination during the pre-litigation procedure
            (Art. EC) (see paras 24-25)
3.                     Freedom of movement for persons – Freedom of establishment – Freedom to provide services – Direct insurance other than life
            assurance and direct life assurance – Directives 92/and 2002/(European Parliament and Council Directive 2002/83, Art.
            14(5); Council Directive 92/49, Art. 12(6)) (see paras 40-42)
Re: 
Failure to fulfil obligations – Failure to fulfil obligations under Article 12(6) of Council Directive 92/49/EEC of June
                  on the coordination of laws, regulations and administrative provisions relating to direct insurance other than life assurance
                  and amending Directives 73/239/EEC and 88/357/EEC (third non-life assurance directive) (OJ L 228, p. 1) and Article 14(5)
                  of Directive 2002/83/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of November concerning life assurance (OJ L 345, p. 1) – National legislation discriminating against insurers from other Member States.
               
Operative part
The Court: 
 
Dismisses the action.
 
Orders the Commission of the European Communities to pay the costs.
