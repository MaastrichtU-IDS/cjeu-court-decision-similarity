
Order of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of May —Corpul Naţional al Poliţiştilor(Case C‑134/12)
Reference for a preliminary ruling — Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union — European Convention on the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms — Validity of national legislation imposing salary reductions on several categories of civil servants — Failure to give effect to European Union law — Clear lack of jurisdiction of the Court of Justice
1.                     References for a preliminary ruling — Jurisdiction of the Court — Limits — Request for interpretation of the Charter of Fundamental
            Rights of the Union — National legislation not amounting to a measure giving effect to Union law — Lack of jurisdiction of
            the Court (Art. TFEU; Charter of fundamental rights of the European Union, Art. 51(1)) (see paras 12, 13, operative part)
2.                     References for a preliminary ruling — Jurisdiction of the Court — Limits — Request for interpretation of the European Convention
            on Human Rights — Lack of jurisdiction of the Court to give a ruling on the interpretation of provisions of international
            law binding the Member States outside the ambit of Union law (Art. TFEU) (see para. 14, operative part)
Re: 
Reference for a preliminary ruling — Curtea de Apel Constanţa — Interpretation of Articles 17(1), and of the Charter
                  of Fundamental Rights of the European Union — Interpretation of Article 15(3) of the Convention on the Protection of Human
                  Rights and Fundamental Freedoms — Lawfulness of national legislation imposing salary reductions on several categories of civil
                  servants — Infringement of the right to property and of the principles of equal treatment and non-discrimination.
               
Operative part 
The Court of Justice of the European Union clearly has no jurisdiction to entertain the reference for a preliminary ruling
         from the Curtea de Apel Constanţa (Romania), made by decision of February 2012.
