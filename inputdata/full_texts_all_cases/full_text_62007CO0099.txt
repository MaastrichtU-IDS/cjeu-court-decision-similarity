
Order of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of May – Smanor and Others v Commission
(Case C‑99/P)
Appeals – Order of the Court of First Instance – Actions for failure to act – Complaints of alleged failure to act by a Member State – Commission not taking a position – Appeal manifestly inadmissible
Appeals – Grounds – Mere repetition of the pleas and arguments put forward before the Court of First Instance – Error of law relied on not identified – Inadmissible (Art. EC; Statute of the Court of Justice, Art. 58, para. 1; Rules of Procedure of the Court, Art. 112(1)(c))
         (see paras 15-17)
      
Re: 
Appeal against the order of the Court of First Instance (Fourth Chamber) of December in Case T-150/Smanor and Others
 v 
Commission
 whereby the Court of First Instance dismissed as inadmissible the action by the applicants for: (1) a declaration that the
                  Commission unlawfully failed to act by not taking a position on the complaints which they had lodged in against the French
                  Republic; and (2) an order for suspension of operation of a judgment and of a decision taken by the national authorities.
               
Operative part:
 
The appeal is dismissed.
 
Smanor SA and Mr & Mrs Ségaud are ordered to bear their own costs.
