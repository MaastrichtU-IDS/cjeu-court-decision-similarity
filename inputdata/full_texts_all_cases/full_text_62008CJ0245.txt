
Judgment of the Court (Eighth Chamber) of March – Commission v Portugal
(Case C‑245/08)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Freedom of movement of persons – Freedom to provide services – Right of establishment – Adjustments consequent on the accession of the Republic of Bulgaria and Romania
1.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of the merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration
            – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see para. 10)
2.                     Freedom of movement for persons – Amendment of secondary legislation by reason of the accession of Bulgaria and Romania –
            Directive 2006/(Art. EC; Council Directive 2006/100) (see paras 9-12, operative part)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Failure to adopt, within the prescribed period, the measures necessary to
                  comply with Council Directive 2006/100/EC of November adapting certain Directives in the field of freedom of movement
                  of persons, by reason of the accession of Bulgaria and Romania (OJ L 363, p. 141).
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by failing to adopt, within the prescribed period, the laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary
                     to comply with Council Directive 2006/100/EC of November adapting certain Directives in the field of freedom of movement
                     of persons, by reason of the accession of Bulgaria and Romania, the Portuguese Republic has failed to fulfil its obligations
                     under Article 2(1) of that directive.
                  
2.
	Orders the Portuguese Republic to pay the costs.
