
Judgment of the Court (Eighth Chamber) of December –Commission v France
(Case C-515/10)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 1999/31/EC – Decision 2003/33/EC – National legislation – Landfill for inert waste – Acceptance of asbestos-cement waste
Environment – Waste – Landfill of waste – Directive 1999/– National legislation providing for the treatment of asbestos-cement waste in landfills for inert waste – Failure to fulfil obligations (Art. TFEU; Council Directive 1999/31, Arts 2(e), 3(1), 6(d) and 16, and Annex II; Council
         Decision 2003/33, Annex) (see paras 25-26, 28, operative part)
      
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil its obligations – Incorrect transposition of Article 2(e), the first subparagraph of Article
                  and Article 6(d) of Council Directive 1999/31/EC of April on the landfill of waste (OJ L 182, p. 1) and the
                  provisions of the Annex to Council Decision 2003/33/EC of December establishing criteria and procedures for the acceptance
                  of waste at landfills pursuant to Article of and Annex II to Directive 1999/31/EC (OJ L 11, p. 27) – National legislation
                  establishing a category of ‘inert and hazardous’ wastes, not in accordance with the directive – Landfill of asbestos-cement
                  waste.
               
Operative part
The Court: 
1.
	Declares that, by failing to adopt the laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary to ensure that asbestos-cement
                     waste is treated in suitable landfills, the French Republic has failed to fulfil its obligations under Article 2(e), the first
                     subparagraph of Article and Article 6(d) of Council Directive 1999/31/EC of April on the landfill of waste and the
                     provisions of the Annex to Council Decision 2003/33/EC of December establishing criteria and procedures for the acceptance
                     of waste at landfills pursuant to Article of and Annex II to Directive 1999/31;
                  
2.
	Orders the French Republic to pay the costs.
