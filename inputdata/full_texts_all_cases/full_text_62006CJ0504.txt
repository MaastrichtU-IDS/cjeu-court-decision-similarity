
Judgment of the Court (First Chamber) of July – Commission v Italy
(Case C‑504/06)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 92/57/EEC –Implementation of minimum safety and health requirements at temporary or mobile construction sites – Article 3(1) – Incorrect transposition
1.                     Actions for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of the merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration
            – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see paras 24-25)
2.                     Social policy – Protection of the safety and health of  workers – Directive 92/on the implementation of minimum safety
            and health requirements at temporary or mobile construction sites (Council Directive 92/57, Art. 3(1)) (see paras 35-36)
3.                     Member States – Obligations – Implementation of directives – Failure to fulfil obligations – National system pleaded as justification
            – Not permissible (Art. EC) (see para. 38)
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Incorrect transposition of Article 3(1) of Council Directive 92/57/EEC of
                  June on the implementation of minimum safety and health requirements at temporary or mobile construction sites (Eighth
                  Individual Directive within the meaning of Article 16(1) of Directive 89/391/EEC) (OJ L 245, p. 6) – Appointment of health
                  and safety coordinators for a construction site where a number of undertakings are operating.
               
Operative part
The Court:
1.
	Declares that, by failing correctly to transpose in Italian law Article 3(1) of Council Directive 92/57/EEC of June on the implementation of minimum safety and health requirements at temporary or mobile construction sites (Eighth Individual
                     Directive within the meaning of Article 16(1) of Directive 89/391/EEC), the Italian Republic has failed to fulfil its obligations
                     under that directive;
                  
2.
	Orders each party to bear its own costs.
