
Judgment of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of January – Commission v Italy
(Case C‑139/04)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Ambient air quality – Limit values
Action for failure to fulfil obligations – Examination of merits by the Court – Situation to be taken into consideration – Situation on expiry of the period laid down in the reasoned opinion (Art. EC) (see para. 13)
Re:
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Infringement of Article of Council Directive 96/62/EC of September
                  concerning ambient air quality assessment and management (OJ L 296, p. 55), in conjunction with Article 4(1) of
                  that directive and Directive 1999/30/EC of April relating to limit values for sulphur dioxide, nitrogen dioxide and
                  oxides of nitrogen, particulate matter and lead in ambient air (OJ L 163, p. 41)
               
Operative part:
The Court:
1.
Declares that, by failing to forward to the Commission of the European Communities for all necessary information concerning
                     the substances subject to Council Directive 1999/30/EC of April relating to limit values for sulphur dioxide, nitrogen
                     dioxide and oxides of nitrogen, particulate matter and lead in ambient air, as required under Article 11(1)(a)(i) and (ii)
                     and (b) of Council Directive 96/62/EC of September concerning ambient air quality assessment and management, the Italian
                     Republic has failed to fulfil its obligations under Article of Directive 96/in conjunction with Article 4(1) of that
                     directive and with Directive 1999/and Article of Commission Decision 2001/839/EC of November laying down a questionnaire
                     to be used for annual reporting on ambient air quality assessment under Directives 96/and 1999/30;
                  
2.
Orders the Italian Republic to pay the costs.
