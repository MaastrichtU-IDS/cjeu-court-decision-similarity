
Judgment of the Court (Sixth Chamber) of January – Commission v Ireland
(Case C‑175/05)
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Directive 92/100/EEC – Copyright – Rental and lending right – Exclusive public lending right – Derogation – Condition of remuneration – Exemption – Scope
Approximation of laws – Copyright and related rights – Protected rental and lending right for works – Directive 92/– Remuneration of authors in the event of public lending (Council Directive 92/100, Arts and 5) (see para. and operative
         part)
      
Re: 
Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations – Breach of Articles and of Council Directive 92/100/EEC of November
                  on rental right and lending right and on certain rights related to copyright in the field of intellectual property (OJ
                  L 346, p. 6) – Derogation from the exclusive public lending right – Scope 
               
Operative part
The Court: 
 
Declares that, by exempting all categories of public lending establishments, within the meaning of Council Directive 92/100/EEC
                     of November on rental right and lending right and on certain rights related to copyright in the field of intellectual
                     property, from the obligation to remunerate authors for the lending carried out by them, Ireland has failed to fulfil its
                     obligations under Articles and of that directive; 
                  
 
Orders Irelands to pay the costs;
 
Orders the Kingdom of Spain to pay its own costs.
